Using the evidence gathered, describe your core personal values.

Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.

I once worked in a factory as an inspector, which had made me kind of unpopular. Im a confident person, and believed that I was doing my job for a reason, so it didnt usually bother me. At a 5am check, I noticed the person I was calibrating smelt of heavily of alcohol. My workplace was mostly maori, and would be considered high safety risk with zero drug and alcohol tolerance.

I thought about my mother, who values honesty. I stopped my check and reported my finding. This resulted in a positive test and disciplinary action for the worker, and some angry co-workers for me.

Eventually people calmed down and realised that I was only doing my job, and that I was consistent. It was hard because I felt like I was selling out as a maori.

At one job, I was influential in organising a cultural night for the 40 different nationalities at our workplace. It was an awesome event and people still ask me when im coming back to organise another one.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

I worked on a project which involved collaboration with another manager for an external sign off. This manager found that the project was not viable and stated the external sign off could not happen. I didnt agree and esculated it, and it got signed off, which was not well recieved.

The strategy I used was to provide cite technical documentation and case studies. It was a project I was passionate about, and I believed the positive outcomes would be worthwhile.

Reflecting back, I would still fight for this project, but I would spend more time marketing it to managers to get them on board. I would invest less in technical information, and more in appealing to the heart.

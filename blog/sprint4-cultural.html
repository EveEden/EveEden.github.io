Neuroplasticity & Growth Mindset

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplaticity benefits people

Neuroplasticity is the ability of the brain to form and reorganize synaptic connections, especially in response to learning or experience or following injury.
It is the concept that our brains have the ability to change, or create new neural connecitons throughout their lifetime. The stimulus for this can be both positive or negative, meaning that positive stimulus can create sucessful learning pathways, and negative stimulus can wire the brain to idea's such as paranoia.
A key concept in this is that Intelligence is not predetermined, so that anyone can learn anything. With the right training, confidence and determination, you can basically do anything you put your mind to.

What are some of the ways to increase your Neuroplaticity

Because Neuroplasticity deals with the biological processes in the brain, it is sensible to say that physical health has benefits for learning. Providing your body with fundamental vitamins and minerals and sleep is important.
It is well known that stress can affect peoples ability to make good descisions, so finding ways to deal with stress is a good way to increase neuroplasticity. An interesting concept is the Amygdala Hyjack or the fight or flight mechanism.
The Amygdala Hijack - Fight or Flight
You can increase Neuroplasticity by challenging ourselves to learn new things - weather its physically by training for a marathon or mentally by understanding how engines work. The act of starting on a path to learning increases our ability to do so.
